How We Created Where is Maverick? — From Idea to Amazon

The real story behind the first book in the Maverick's Adventures series. Not the polished version — the actual process, including what we did not expect and what we would do differently.

Behind the Scenes Children's Books Self Publishing

Every book starts somewhere. For Where is Maverick? it started with a cockapoo who had an inexplicable talent for disappearing into the smallest, most inconvenient spaces at the most inopportune moments.

Maverick has always done this. He finds a spot — behind the sofa, under a blanket, wedged behind a plant pot in the garden — and waits, apparently thrilled with himself, while everyone else looks for him. It is genuinely funny every single time. And at some point the thought arrived: this is a children's book. A real one. A proper hide-and-seek story with a real dog at the centre of it.

That thought sat around for a while, as thoughts do. Then we decided to actually do something with it.

The Process — How the Book Got Made

Step 01

Writing the story

The brief was simple: a rhyming hide-and-seek story, age appropriate for 2-6 year olds, with Maverick as the star. The rhyming format came naturally — it makes read-alouds more enjoyable for parents and helps young children follow along. We went through several drafts to get the rhythm right and make sure every page had the right balance of text and space for illustration.

Step 02

Creating the illustrations

The illustrations were one of the most important decisions. A children's book lives or dies on its artwork. We wanted vibrant, warm and age-appropriate illustrations that brought Maverick's character to life — curious, playful, instantly loveable. The finished illustrations gave the book exactly the feel we were hoping for.

Step 03

Publishing on Amazon KDP

We used Amazon KDP (Kindle Direct Publishing) to publish — both paperback and Kindle editions. KDP is free to use, handles printing on demand for physical copies and makes the book available globally on Amazon automatically. The process took around 72 hours from submission to the book being live. No upfront printing costs, no warehouse, no minimum order quantities.

Step 04

Building the brand around Maverick

The book was always going to be part of something bigger. We built the Maverick's Adventures Instagram, the website and the Blogger alongside the book launch — creating a space where the series could grow, where we could share Maverick's real adventures and where readers could follow along for Book 2 news and free activity packs.
